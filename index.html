José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda June 19, 1861

José Rizal (1861-1896) is one of the most revered figures in Philippine history. He was a multifaceted intellectual and a political activist, best known for his political writings that inspired the Philippine revolution and ultimately led to his execution by the Spanish colonizers.
Emilio Aguinaldo y Famy March 22, 1869

Emilio Aguinaldo (born March 22/23, 1869, near Cavite, Luzon, Philippines—died February 6, 1964, Quezon City) was a Filipino leader and politician who fought first against Spain and later against the United States for the independence of the Philippines.
Marcelo Hilario del Pilar y Gatmaitán August 30, 1850

del Pilar and also known by his nom de plume Pláridel, was a Filipino writer, lawyer, journalist, and freemason. Del Pilar, along with José Rizal and Graciano López Jaena, became known as the leaders of the Reform Movement in Spain. Del Pilar was born and brought up in Bulakan, Bulacan.
Andrés Bonifacio y de Castro Nov. 30, 1863

Andres Bonifacio (1863-1897) was the founder of the Katipunan, a militant nationalist secret society that led the rebellion against Spain. Bonifacio declared Philippine independence in 1896 in the wake of the execution of his hero and mentor José Rizal.
María Josefa Gabriela Cariño March 19, 1731
